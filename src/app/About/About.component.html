Rolls-Royce Motor Cars

Rolls-Royce Motor Cars Limited is a British luxury automobile maker which has operated as a wholly owned subsidiary of BMW AG since 2003 as the exclusive manufacturer of Rolls-Royce-branded motor cars. The company's line of vehicles includes the Phantom, a four-door saloon first offered in 2003 as well as its extended wheelbase two-door coupé and convertible variants; the smaller Ghost four-door saloon; Wraith two-door coupe; Dawn convertible; the Cullinan SUV, and the forthcoming 2023 Spectre,

he first all-electric Rolls-Royce. In 1998, Vickers decided to sell Rolls-Royce Motors. The most likely buyer was BMW, which already supplied engines and other components for Rolls-Royce and Bentley cars, but BMW's final offer of £340 million was beaten by Volkswagen's £430 million ($703 million).

For over a century, Rolls-Royce has been synonymous with the ultimate in automotive luxury. From our earliest days crafting cars for the world's most discerning customers, to our current lineup of exceptional models that combine cutting-edge technology with traditional hand-crafted techniques, we have always been committed to delivering an unparalleled driving experience.

At Rolls-Royce, we believe that luxury is about more than just the materials and features that go into our cars. It's about the experience of owning a Rolls-Royce, and the sense of pride and connection that comes with being part of our exclusive community of owners. From the iconic Spirit of Ecstasy hood ornament to the sumptuous leather interiors and hand-polished wood veneers, every Rolls-Royce is a masterpiece of design and engineering. We use only the finest materials and the most advanced technology to create cars that are both breathtakingly beautiful and remarkably powerful. But at Rolls-Royce, we also understand that luxury is about service as well as style.

That's why we offer our customers a range of personalized services and exclusive experiences, from bespoke customization options to private tours of our factory in Goodwood, England.
